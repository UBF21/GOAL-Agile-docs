# Level 1 — GOAL Practitioner (GP)

## Overview

The GOAL Practitioner certification is the entry point into the GOAL credentialing system. It is designed for individuals who are actively participating in a GOAL-based team in any role — as a Delivery Team member, a Product Strategist, or a practitioner beginning to function as a Flow Master. The credential demonstrates that the holder understands the fundamental principles, mechanics, and artifacts of the GOAL framework well enough to operate effectively as a contributing team member.

---

## Target Audience

The GP certification is appropriate for:

- Software engineers, developers, QA engineers, UX designers, and other technical contributors participating in GOAL teams
- Product managers, product owners, and business analysts operating in the Product Strategist role
- Scrum Masters, project managers, or team leads transitioning into a Flow Master function
- Practitioners with experience in Scrum or Kanban who are adopting GOAL
- New graduates or early-career professionals joining organizations that operate GOAL

The GP is **not** appropriate for:
- Individuals seeking to lead a GOAL implementation without having participated in one
- Individuals who have not yet had any exposure to the GOAL methodology

---

## Prerequisites

To be eligible to sit the GP examination, candidates must meet ALL of the following:

**Experience requirement:**
A minimum of three (3) months of active participation in a team practicing GOAL. Active participation is defined as contributing to work that appears on a Flow Board, attending Daily Flow Syncs, and participating in at least one complete Goal Cycle from Smart Planning through Data-Driven Retrospective.

**Acceptable evidence (one of the following required):**
- A signed attestation from a certified GFMC or GPL who directly observed the candidate's participation
- Employment records showing the candidate's role in a GOAL team
- A letter from the team lead or manager confirming three months of participation

**Prior certification:** None — the GP is the entry-level certification.

**Training requirement:** Completion of at least 8 hours of formal GOAL training from an accredited provider, OR documented self-study of the GOAL Methodology documentation at v0.2 or higher.

## Knowledge Domains

| Domain | Description | Exam Weight |
|--------|-------------|:-----------:|
| Domain 1: GOAL Foundations | Manifesto, core principles, comparison with Scrum and Kanban, anti-pattern recognition | 20% |
| Domain 2: Roles and Responsibilities | Flow Master, Product Strategist, Delivery Team — responsibilities, authorities, and boundaries | 15% |
| Domain 3: Workflow and Artifacts | Flow Board mechanics, Goal Cycle structure, all six artifacts | 30% |
| Domain 4: Events and Protocols | All five events, Interrupt Management Protocol, Blocked Task Protocol, WIP enforcement | 25% |
| Domain 5: Metrics and Value | Core flow metrics, 3-Layer Value Framework, when and how to use data | 10% |

### Domain Detail

**Domain 1 — GOAL Foundations (20%)**
- The five values of the GOAL Manifesto
- The eight core principles
- The problem statement GOAL addresses relative to Scrum and Kanban
- Recognition of anti-patterns and healthy vs failed GOAL practice
- What GOAL does and does not prescribe

**Domain 2 — Roles and Responsibilities (15%)**
- The three primary roles and their distinct responsibilities
- The difference between a Flow Master and a Scrum Master
- Authority boundaries for each role
- The Backlog Curator function
- What self-management means for the Delivery Team
- How roles interact during each event

**Domain 3 — Workflow and Artifacts (30%)**
- All six standard Flow Board columns and two special lanes, plus the Technical Debt lane
- Goal Cycle structure and all three termination modes (normal, early, extended)
- Goal stability and scope flexibility rules
- Task sizing (Small, Medium, Large) and the 3-day maximum rule
- All six GOAL artifacts and their content requirements
- The two-level Definition of Done (Baseline vs Extended)
- DoD vs Acceptance Criteria distinction
- Backlog item lifecycle (Discovery through Archive)
- Acceptance criteria format (Given/When/Then)
- WIP formula and Focus Factor selection

**Domain 4 — Events and Protocols (25%)**
- All five events: purpose, duration, participants, and agenda
- The three interrupt classification levels (P1, P2, P3) and their handling procedures
- Expedite lane rules and authorization requirements
- The Blocked Task Protocol: required Block Register information, blocker type categories, escalation timeline
- Conditions under which a cycle can close early
- The WIP enforcement protocol and the priority order when WIP is full

**Domain 5 — Metrics and Value (10%)**
- Definitions of all core metrics: Flow Time, Cycle Time, Wait Time, Flow Efficiency, Block Rate, Delivery Rate, Focus Ratio, Cycle Accuracy Index
- How to calculate Flow Efficiency from given data
- Benchmark ranges for Flow Efficiency and Block Rate
- The 3-Layer Value Framework: Business Value, User Value, Technical Value
- How value tagging is applied at Smart Planning and measured at Goal Review

## Practical Requirements

In addition to the written examination, GP candidates must submit a **Cycle Participation Record** demonstrating active engagement with GOAL practice. This is a pass/fail eligibility gate (not a scored component).

**Cycle Participation Record requirements:**
- Participation in a Smart Planning Session for at least one cycle
- Attendance at a minimum of 5 Daily Flow Syncs during the cycle
- At least one task completed through the full Definition of Done process
- Participation in the Goal Review at cycle end
- Participation in the Data-Driven Retrospective

The Cycle Participation Record is a short-form document (maximum 2 pages) completed by the candidate and countersigned by the team's Flow Master or a certified GFMC/GPL.

---

## Assessment Format

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Exam type | Computer-based, proctored, closed-book |
| Number of questions | 75 |
| Duration | 90 minutes |
| Multiple choice (1 correct answer) | 55 questions |
| Multiple select (2+ correct answers) | 15 questions |
| Scenario-based (single best answer) | 5 questions |

**Language:** English (primary). Portuguese and Spanish within 12 months of program launch.

---

## Passing Criteria

| Component | Minimum Required |
|-----------|:----------------:|
| Overall examination score | 70% (53 of 75 questions) |
| Domain 1 — GOAL Foundations | 60% minimum |
| Domain 2 — Roles and Responsibilities | 60% minimum |
| Domain 3 — Workflow and Artifacts | 60% minimum |
| Domain 4 — Events and Protocols | 60% minimum |
| Domain 5 — Metrics and Value | 60% minimum |
| Cycle Participation Record | Accepted (pass/fail) |

A candidate who meets the overall minimum but fails the domain minimum in any single domain does not pass.

**Retake policy:**
- First retake: eligible 14 days after the failed attempt
- Second retake: eligible 30 days after the first retake
- Third+ retakes: eligible 90 days after previous attempt; requires evidence of additional study

---

## Certification Validity and Renewal

**Validity period:** 2 years from date of passing

**Renewal:** Complete 20 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) within the 2-year validity period. Renewal does not require re-sitting the examination if the credential has not expired.

**Expired certifications:** Must re-sit the full GP examination and submit a new Cycle Participation Record.

---

## What GP Credential Holders Are Authorized to Do

- Represent themselves as having passed the GOAL Practitioner examination
- List the GP designation on professional profiles
- Participate in Institute-recognized GP communities and peer networks
- Apply for the GFMC examination (subject to GFMC prerequisites)

GP holders are **not** authorized to deliver GOAL training courses on behalf of the Institute or coach teams as a GOAL-certified coach (the GCT track is required for this).
