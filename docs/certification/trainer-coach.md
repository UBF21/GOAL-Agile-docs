# GOAL Certified Trainer (GCT)

## Overview

The GOAL Certified Trainer is a specialist track designed for practitioners who wish to formally train others in the GOAL methodology. This track runs parallel to the practitioner progression — it is not a higher level than the GPL but a specialist designation for a specific function: delivering GOAL training.

The GCT designation authorizes the holder to deliver Institute-recognized GOAL training programs and officially coach teams through GOAL adoption. It is the only credential that permits using the GOAL Institute's training materials and branding in commercial training delivery.

---

## Who Needs the GCT

The GCT is required for:
- Trainers or facilitators delivering paid GOAL training courses
- Coaches formally engaged to lead a GOAL adoption in an organization
- Internal trainers at organizations who will train other internal teams on GOAL
- Consultants who include GOAL training as part of their service offering

The GCT is **not** required to:
- Practice GOAL on your own team
- Coach your own team informally
- Use GOAL methodology documentation for self-directed learning

---

## Prerequisites

To be eligible for the GCT assessment, candidates must meet ALL of the following:

**Prior certification:** Active GP certification at minimum. GFMC is strongly recommended and may be required for specific GCT authorization scopes (see Authorization Levels below).

**Training delivery experience:** Demonstrated experience delivering learning content to groups. This includes formal training sessions, facilitated workshops, internal knowledge-sharing sessions, or conference presentations. Minimum 10 hours of documented delivery experience.

**GOAL practice experience:** At minimum the GP prerequisite experience (3 months active GOAL participation). Candidates who only hold GP may not train beyond introductory content; GFMC is required to train on Flow Master role content.

---

## GCT Authorization Levels

The GCT credential has two authorization levels based on the candidate's practitioner credentials:

| Authorization Level | Required Practitioner Credential | Training Scope |
|--------------------|----------------------------------|----------------|
| GCT Foundation | GP (minimum) | GOAL Foundations, Manifesto, Basic Events, Introduction to Artifacts |
| GCT Advanced | GFMC (required) | All GOAL content including Flow Master role, metrics, protocol execution, and retrospective facilitation |

Candidates with GP only receive GCT Foundation authorization. To expand to GCT Advanced, the candidate must first earn the GFMC.

## Assessment Process

The GCT assessment has three components:

### Component 1: Methodology Examination

A written exam focused on the trainer's ability to explain GOAL concepts accurately and answer participant questions:

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Number of questions | 60 |
| Duration | 75 minutes |
| Focus | Teaching accuracy, common misconceptions, facilitating understanding |
| Passing score | 75% overall, 65% per domain |

The methodology examination is not the same as the GP exam. It tests whether the candidate can explain concepts clearly, anticipate common misunderstandings, and handle typical participant questions about GOAL.

### Component 2: Training Portfolio

A portfolio demonstrating experience delivering adult learning content. Requirements:
- Minimum 2 training/facilitation samples (session plans, slide decks, workshop designs)
- Evidence of delivery (attendee count, feedback summaries, session duration)
- At least one example that specifically covers GOAL content

Portfolio is reviewed pass/fail by a GCT assessor.

### Component 3: Observed Training Delivery

The core assessment: the candidate delivers a 45–60 minute GOAL training session to a live audience (minimum 4 participants) with a certified GCT assessor observing.

**Evaluation dimensions (each rated 1–4):**
1. Content accuracy — Is GOAL content taught correctly per the official methodology?
2. Clarity — Are explanations clear and appropriate for the audience's experience level?
3. Facilitation — Does the trainer create engagement and handle questions well?
4. Pacing and structure — Is the session well-organized and within time bounds?
5. Anti-pattern handling — Does the trainer correctly address common misapplications?

Passing requirement: Score of 3 or higher on Content Accuracy, and average 2.5 or higher across all dimensions.

---

## Certification Validity and Renewal

**Validity:** 2 years

**Renewal:** 30 CEUs including at least 10 hours of actual training delivery within the 2-year period. No re-assessment is required if the underlying practitioner credential (GP or GFMC) is also current.

**Expired:** If the GCT expires, the candidate must complete a new Observed Training Delivery assessment. The portfolio requirement is waived for renewal if the credential expired within 12 months.

---

## What GCT Holders Are Authorized to Do

**GCT Foundation holders:**
- Deliver Institute-recognized GOAL Foundation training (introductory content)
- Use the GOAL Institute's Foundation training materials with attribution
- Market themselves as GOAL Certified Trainer (Foundation) on professional profiles
- Access the Institute's trainer resource library (Foundation tier)

**GCT Advanced holders:**
- Deliver all Institute-recognized GOAL training programs
- Use all GOAL Institute training materials with attribution
- Deliver GP exam preparation workshops
- Coach teams through full GOAL adoption as a GOAL-certified coach
- Access the Institute's trainer resource library (all tiers)
- List in the Institute's public directory of authorized GOAL trainers

---

## Certification Ethics Requirements

All GCT holders must:
- Represent GOAL methodology accurately and completely, not selectively
- Not modify or customize official GOAL content in ways that conflict with the methodology
- Disclose their certification status and any limitations of their authorization scope
- Not claim credentials or authorizations they do not hold
- Report any conflicts of interest when assessing or certifying others

Violations of the ethics code may result in suspension or revocation of the GCT designation.
