# Level 2 — GOAL Flow Master Certified (GFMC)

## Overview

The GOAL Flow Master Certified designation is the professional-level credential for individuals serving in the Flow Master role — the central operational leadership role in a GOAL team. Where the GP validates that a practitioner understands how GOAL works, the GFMC validates that a practitioner can **run a GOAL team with skill**: using flow data to diagnose problems, enforcing protocols under organizational pressure, and leading the continuous improvement cycle.

The GFMC is an operational excellence credential, not a management credential.

---

## Target Audience

The GFMC is appropriate for:

- Current Flow Masters seeking formal validation of their competency
- Scrum Masters, Kanban practitioners, or team leads transitioning into the Flow Master role
- Agile coaches specializing in the GOAL methodology
- Technical leads or engineering managers operating as de facto Flow Masters
- Individuals preparing to formally assume the Flow Master role

---

## Prerequisites

To be eligible to sit the GFMC examination, candidates must meet ALL of the following:

**Prior certification:** Active GP certification (not expired at time of GFMC application)

**Flow Master experience:** A minimum of six (6) months of active experience in the Flow Master role, or a substantially equivalent role (monitoring and managing flow on a GOAL board, facilitating Daily Flow Syncs, enforcing WIP limits, and facilitating Data-Driven Retrospectives)

**Completed cycles:** A minimum of six (6) complete Goal Cycles facilitated as Flow Master or equivalent. "Facilitated" means: led the Smart Planning Session, monitored the cycle's flow, and co-facilitated the Retrospective.

**Portfolio:** Candidates must submit and have accepted a Flow Master Portfolio before sitting the examination.

## Knowledge Domains

| Domain | Description | Exam Weight |
|--------|-------------|:-----------:|
| Domain 1: Advanced GOAL Mechanics | Edge cases, judgment calls, deep protocol application | 20% |
| Domain 2: Flow Master Role Mastery | Authority, facilitation competency, protecting team flow, conflict handling | 20% |
| Domain 3: Flow Metrics and Diagnosis | Interpreting metric patterns, diagnosing systemic problems, data-based improvement | 25% |
| Domain 4: Protocol Execution Under Pressure | Managing interruptions, escalating blockers, WIP violations, cycle extensions | 20% |
| Domain 5: Facilitation and Retrospective Leadership | Running retrospectives that produce measurable improvement | 10% |
| Domain 6: Anti-Patterns and Corrective Action | Identifying, diagnosing, and correcting GOAL anti-patterns | 5% |

### Domain Highlights

**Domain 3 — Flow Metrics and Diagnosis (25% — highest weighted)**

This domain reflects that metric fluency is the core competency that distinguishes a skilled Flow Master. Topics include:
- Reading all seven metrics in combination, not in isolation
- Identifying what metric patterns indicate which systemic problems
- Distinguishing between statistical anomaly and genuine trend
- Calculating all metrics from raw data (candidates must work with numbers)
- Diagnosing why improvement has stalled on a plateaued team

**Domain 4 — Protocol Execution Under Pressure (20%)**

Covers protocols in realistic, politically challenging scenarios:
- P1/P2/P3 classification consistency when stakeholders are applying pressure
- Conducting a cycle extension with all documentation and stakeholder communication
- Managing a 48-hour blocked task through formal escalation, including when the blocker involves a senior stakeholder
- Enforcing the single-Expedite-task rule when a second "urgent" request arrives

## Flow Master Portfolio (Practical Requirement)

The Flow Master Portfolio is a structured evidence submission required before sitting the examination. It is reviewed for completeness, authenticity, and sufficient depth — not scored numerically.

**Section A — Cycle Records (required)**
Summaries of a minimum of 4 of the 6 required Goal Cycles, each including:
- Cycle dates and duration (estimated vs actual, with CAI entry)
- Team size and primary goals
- Flow Efficiency and Block Rate for the cycle
- One significant flow issue and how it was handled
- One retrospective improvement and whether it was measurably effective

Each summary: 300–500 words.

**Section B — Protocol Application Evidence (required)**
Documentation of at least 2 real situations in which the candidate applied one of: Interrupt Management Protocol, Blocked Task Protocol escalation (at/beyond 24-hour mark), or Cycle Extension.

For each: describe circumstances, action taken, GOAL protocol followed, and outcome. Each account: 400–600 words.

**Section C — Metrics Analysis (required)**
A written analysis of a real team's flow metrics over at minimum 3 consecutive cycles:
- Metrics for each cycle (Flow Efficiency, Block Rate, Cycle Time trend)
- Patterns identified and what they indicate
- One specific improvement identified from data and the action taken
- Whether the improvement had a measurable effect

Length: 600–900 words. Raw metric data in appendix.

**Section D — Retrospective Evidence (required)**
Summary of a Data-Driven Retrospective facilitated by the candidate:
- Metrics reviewed
- 1–3 improvements identified
- Success metrics defined
- Whether improvements were tracked in subsequent cycles

Length: 300–400 words.

## Assessment Format

**Written Examination:**

| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Exam type | Computer-based, proctored, closed-book |
| Number of questions | 110 |
| Duration | 150 minutes |
| Multiple choice (1 correct) | 70 questions |
| Multiple select (2+ correct) | 25 questions |
| Scenario-based with analysis | 15 questions |

**Practical Case Study (after passing written exam):**
- Released upon passing the written examination
- Must be submitted within 14 calendar days
- Approximately 4–6 pages of scenario material describing a GOAL team over 3 cycles
- Raw metric data for each cycle
- 8–12 structured response questions requiring analysis, protocol application, and recommendations
- Expected submission length: 1,500–2,500 words
- Evaluated by a panel of two GPL reviewers

**Case Study evaluation dimensions:**
1. Accuracy (Is the methodological application correct?)
2. Reasoning (Is the analysis logical and grounded in data?)
3. Practicality (Are recommendations realistic and actionable?)
4. Completeness (Are all questions fully addressed?)

Must score 3 or higher on Accuracy and average 2.5 or higher across all four dimensions to pass.

---

## Passing Criteria

| Component | Minimum Required |
|-----------|:----------------:|
| Overall examination score | 75% (83 of 110 questions) |
| All domains | 65% minimum per domain |
| Practical Case Study | Pass (rubric evaluation) |
| Flow Master Portfolio | Accepted (prior to exam) |

A candidate who passes the exam but fails the case study must retake only the case study (not the full exam) within 90 days.

---

## Certification Validity and Renewal

**Validity:** 2 years

**Renewal:** 30 CEUs within 2-year period, with at least 15 CEUs from GFMC-designated content (advanced facilitation, metrics application, or protocol management). Must also submit a brief Activity Summary (max 1 page) confirming continued active practice.

**Expired:** Must re-sit full GFMC examination (written + case study) and resubmit an updated Flow Master Portfolio.

---

## What GFMC Holders Are Authorized to Do

In addition to all GP-level authorizations:

- Sign and countersign Cycle Participation Records for GP candidates
- Formally attest to GP and GFMC experience prerequisites for candidates they have directly supervised
- Apply for participation in the Institute's GFMC Practitioner Network
- Apply for the GPL examination (subject to GPL prerequisites)
- Identify themselves publicly as GOAL Flow Master Certified (GFMC designation)

GFMC holders are **not** authorized to deliver Institute-recognized GOAL training without also holding the GCT designation.
